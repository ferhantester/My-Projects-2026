Problem Statement¶

Business Context¶

Healthcare workers — doctors, nurses, and specialists — deal with enormous amounts of medical information every single day. To treat a patient well, they need the right information at the right time. But right now, that information is spread across hundreds of research papers, medical manuals, and different systems. Finding what you need, when you need it, is genuinely hard.

This becomes even more critical in emergencies. When a patient's life is on the line, a doctor cannot afford to spend precious minutes searching through piles of data. They need fast, reliable answers they can trust.

The bigger problem is that the volume of medical knowledge keeps growing every year. No single person can read and remember everything. So even the most experienced doctors can sometimes miss something important simply because they were not aware of it or could not find it in time.

The good news is that this is a solvable problem. If hospitals and healthcare centers bring all their key medical knowledge into one centralized, easy-to-use system, it changes everything. Doctors get quick access to trusted, up-to-date information without having to dig around. Decision-making becomes faster and more confident. Patient care improves naturally as a result.

In short, healthcare professionals are not lacking in skill or dedication. What they often lack is a smarter, simpler way to access the knowledge they already need. Fixing that one thing can make a significant difference in how patients are treated and how healthcare teams operate every day.

Questions to Answer

  1. What is the protocol for managing sepsis in a critical care unit?
  2. What are the common symptoms of appendicitis, and can it be cured via medicine? If not, what surgical procedure should be followed to treat it?
  3. What are the effective treatments or solutions for addressing sudden patchy hair loss, commonly seen as localized bald spots on the scalp, and what could be the possible causes behind it?
  4. What treatments are recommended for a person who has sustained a physical injury to brain tissue, resulting in temporary or permanent impairment of brain function?
  5. What are the necessary precautions and treatment steps for a person who has fractured their leg during a hiking trip, and what should be considered for their care and recovery?

Objective¶

As an AI specialist, the job here is to build a smart AI system that helps doctors and healthcare workers find medical information faster and more accurately. The system will be based on a technique called RAG — which simply means the AI doesn't just rely on what it already knows, but actively pulls up relevant information from trusted medical books and manuals when answering a question.

The starting point is understanding the real problem. Healthcare workers are drowning in information. There are thousands of medical guidelines, research papers, and reference manuals out there. No one can read all of it, and searching through it manually takes time that doctors simply don't have. The AI system is designed to solve exactly this — acting like a knowledgeable assistant that instantly finds the right answer from the right source.

Once the problem is clear, the next step is applying the right AI techniques to make decision-making smoother and faster. Instead of a doctor spending an hour searching, they ask a question in plain language and get a precise, sourced answer in seconds. This is especially powerful during diagnoses, where having the right information quickly can directly improve what treatment a patient receives.

Beyond individual diagnoses, this kind of system also has the potential to bring consistency to healthcare. When every doctor in a hospital — or even across multiple hospitals — is pulling answers from the same trusted sources, the standard of care naturally becomes more uniform. Fewer mistakes happen because of outdated or missing knowledge.

Finally, the goal is not just to talk about this idea but to actually build a working prototype that proves it works. A real, functional demo that shows how a doctor can ask a medical question, get a reliable answer drawn from established medical manuals, and use that to make a better, faster decision for their patient.

Data Description¶

The Merck Manuals are medical references published by the American pharmaceutical company Merck & Co., that cover a wide range of medical topics, including disorders, tests, diagnoses, and drugs. The manuals have been published since 1899, when Merck & Co. was still a subsidiary of the German company Merck.

The manual is provided as a PDF with over 4,000 pages divided into 23 sections.

# Function to generate answer using Mistral-7B-Instruct
# Uses Mistral's recommended [INST] prompt format
def generate_response(question):

    # Mistral instruct format: [INST] ... [/INST]
    prompt = f"""[INST] You are a medical assistant AI.

Give clear, accurate, and structured answers.
Use simple language and bullet points where possible.

Question: {question} [/INST]"""

    output = llm(
        prompt,
        temperature=0.2,      # Low = more accurate, less hallucination
        max_tokens=300,       # Sufficient for complete answers
        stop=["[INST]", "</s>"]  # Stop tokens to prevent runaway generation
    )

    return output["choices"][0]["text"].strip()

The function uses the Mistral [INST] instruction format with stop tokens to prevent repetition loops. Low temperature (0.2) ensures factual, consistent medical responses.

def rag_generate(context, question, temperature=0.2, max_tokens=300):
    """Generate an answer grounded in the retrieved context using Mistral."""

    prompt = f"""[INST] You are a medical assistant.

Answer ONLY using the provided context below.
If the answer is not in the context, say "Not in context."
Be concise and use bullet points where appropriate.

Context:
{context}

Question: {question} [/INST]"""

    output = llm(
        prompt,
        temperature=temperature,
        max_tokens=max_tokens,
        stop=["[INST]", "</s>"]
    )

    return output["choices"][0]["text"].strip()
